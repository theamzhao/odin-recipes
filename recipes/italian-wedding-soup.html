Italian Wedding Soup

This dish is a Napoli family favorite and something I have incorporated into my regular rotation of meals - it's especially warming in the winter and saves for the rest of the week if I am having a busy schedule. I especially love the mini meatballs and small pasta. Orzo and couscoussaki are my go-tos.

Ingredients

    Meatballs

  • 8oz lean ground beef
  • 8oz ground pork
  • 1/2 cup fresh hearty bread crumbs
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1.5 tsp minced fresh oregano
  • 1/2 cup finely shredded parmesan
  • 1 large egg
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

    Soup

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 1/4 cups 1/4-inch diced carrots
  • 1 1/4 cups diced yellow onion
  • 3/4 cup 1/4-inch diced celery
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced (1.5 Tbsp)
  • 5 (14.5 oz) cans low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 cup dry acini de pepe or orzo pasta
  • 6 oz fresh spinach or kale, chopped
  • Finely shredded parmesan, for serving
  • Lemon wedge, for serving

Instructions

  1. Step 1:

    For the meatballs: Add beef and pork to a large mixing bowl. Add in bread crumbs, parsley, oregano, parmesan, egg, 1 tsp salt and 1/4 tsp pepper. Gently toss and break up mixture with hands to evenly coat and distribute. Shape mixture into very small meatballs, about 3/4 inch to 1 inch and transfer to a large plate.

  2. Step 2:

    Heat 1 Tbsp olive oil in a large non-stick skillet over medium-high heat. Add half of the meatballs and cook until browned, turning occasionally (to brown on 2 or 3 sides), about 4 minutes total.

  3. Step 3:

    Transfer meatballs to a plate lined with paper towels while leaving oil in skillet. Repeat process with remaining meatballs (note that meatballs won't be cooked through at this point, they'll continue to cook through in the soup).

  4. Step 4:

    For the soup: While meatballs are browning, heat 1 Tbsp olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add carrots, onions and celery and saute until veggies have softened about 6 - 8 minutes, add garlic and saute 1 minute longer.

  5. Step 5:

    Pour in chicken broth, season soup with salt and pepper to taste and bring mixture to a boil. Add in pasta and meatballs, reduce heat to light boil (about medium or medium-low).

  6. Step 6:

    Cover and cook, stirring occasionally until pasta is tender and meatballs have cooked through, about 10 minutes, while adding in spinach during the last minute of cooking. Serve warm, sprinkle each serving with parmesan cheese.
